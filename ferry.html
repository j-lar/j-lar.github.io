On the ferry to Kingston, desperate
for peace, we stand on the foredeck and watch
the crooked line of the peninsula
approaching. February's wind is sharp
and stings your face like tears held back too long.
I feel as impotent against the storm
inside you as I am against the wind
and rain outside, but some things have to run
their course before we find the peace we need.
I love the wind for blowing like I love
the peace that comes, unbidden, to the tree
that holds the storm until it's spent, and holds
the peace and finds it sweeter for the storm
that gives itself to him.
