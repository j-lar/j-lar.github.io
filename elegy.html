Elegy

When you showed me the guns I was afraid
to hold them. I was curious how it felt
to hold the cold steel in my hand, the dead
weight pulling toward the ground with logic born
of power, the logic of death pulling

my body down. I know it weighed on his
mind too. The sunlight in the winter sky
flashed sharp on guns and glass as we pushed shut
the canopy and turned away to leave.
The knowledge of good and evil rested

for some time in our hearts and mouths. We tried
to wash it down with beer, but sleep came hard
that night. And that last day, shells littering
the muddy ground like broken promises,
did the powder burnt barrel taste like fruit?
