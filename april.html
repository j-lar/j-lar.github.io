In April, I turn toward summer and toward
watermelon, green foothills to blue mountains,
and rivers flowing seaward. Children's voices
float past the window, slipping syllables
inside my room from time to time. I don't know
what happens next, but, then, I usually don't.
The sky is white. Clouds whitewash the ceiling
of this in-between space I'm living in,
halfway between the valley and the sea,
those places where, in dreams, I see myself,
the places where I want to be. But now,
we float in seas of our own making, tides
and continents the traffic of our lives.
It took ten years to build the Suez; thirty-
four to join Atlantic and Pacific
in Panama, the Isthmus of Darien
at long last joining water with water,
a commerce made more sweet for years of waiting.
And North and South shift in their beds and reach
to find another, better, way to meet.
