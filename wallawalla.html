Walla Walla

Jason says the lake looks like land
from up here. It’s hard to see the valley
any way but whole. The patchwork quilt of
brown and green and yellow covers bones
(valleys are feminine, hills are too);
history lies silently beside
mothers, grandmothers, and great-grandmothers.
Men grow in them and are borne by them and
die in them. Beating hard against the
soil they sink into her arms to rest.
When it’s finished they will look the same, like
land and water. Land needs water and
water seeks the land to cover history.
