From the Frontier of Living

Apologies to Tony Hoagland

The emptiness and plenty of this valley
while we stand by the road, the sheriff looks
at you and Kate and me and follows our eyes

down the hill; we all see the lights from the
penitentiary that blink with envy
in the twilight over Walla Walla

and everybody's high but the officer,
who looks at us and doesn't blink a lid
at three barefoot college students and their car–

gas tank empty, red eyes and unkempt hair,
and drives us into town, the dank smell
of sweat and hills like incense in the air.
