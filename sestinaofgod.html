Sestina of God


Eve and Adam camp outside the garden.
Stripping off their skins, they nightswim naked,
knowing that they’ve gone too far to come back
now. The moonlight makes them bold and, curious
who or what is living in their past,
they walk beside the wall–was it a wall

that kept them out of paradise? Not a wall;
angels dancing rings around the garden,
heads of pins like pearls as they passed.
And in between each dancer’s step God’s naked
eye appears. Each step reveals his curious
glance into their eyes and time stares back

dark and cold. God stares and turns His back,
stares again in fits and starts and time’s wall,
growing without effort, makes him curious.
God looks out from just inside the garden.
Abel kills a lamb, God faints, lies naked
on the grass, wakes to see Noah is passed

out in his tent. God faints, wakes, and Lot’s passed
out, his wife stuck looking back
at angels dancing sadly. Lot is naked.
God struggles with time. Now the wall
is gone. The earth is young. God’s in the garden
hanging out the clothes. Eve is curious

tonight. She goes walking. Adam’s curious,
too. God sleeps in fits as time creeps past
His windowsill. Meanwhile, back in the garden,
Adam coughs and thinks about a wall
to know what’s in from out, and God is naked.

God awakes, and now the earth is naked.
A garden appears inside his curious
mind. It grows and now he sees this wall
between us; he sees that you and I have passed
out of time. God faints. He wakes. He’s back
on his knees, pulling weeds in the garden.

Back when God was naked we were curious.
Time passed and stared and now God clothes his back
with earth, and there’s a wall around this garden.
