South on 95 and half a pack of cigarettes left. Ahead
the confluence of state and interstate
crashes like my father's church. On the horizon,
wildfires glow red like prophecy
but Reno 150 miles isn't prophecy, and the ash filling
the sky looks more like history.
I love the road like my father loves his church,
but I don't have to lead anyone anywhere tonight.
