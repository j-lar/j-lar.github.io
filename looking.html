Looking for a way into a  poem,

I remember that time, as a boy
growing into my skin--all ankles
and elbows and knees and unexpected
erections--when nothing seemed to fit.

Later, trying to get back what I had
lost, I find the words, too late to make them
fit, to tell you what is true. The words seem
empty without context, without your ears.

Looking for a way into a poem,
I remember Jacob waiting seven
years for inspiration, waiting seven
more for truth; having to live with both.
