Cannon Beach

There’s something about these silences of old
friends that leave nothing unsaid, when
everything has been done, but we’ll be damned
if we’re going to let that stop us now, not
without a fight anyway. You’re driving down
101 and the rain is pounding us like the sea
pounds the shore. And I don’t know why,
but I’m thinking about that time you punched me
right in the face. We were playing kickball after church
and you punched me. Right in the face.
You broke my sunglasses you crazy bastard,
those neon plastic kind. Now we ride in silence
with years of future behind, the sea and eternity
just outside the passenger window, and somewhere
between the tires and earth: history.
And beauty shimmers just ahead on the asphalt,
but we don’t notice, because truth always seems to hover
just beyond our grasp anyway. That’s how we like it
because it never looks the same when we’re standing
still. Drive, brother, drive, and let’s not look back until
tomorrow doesn’t come.
